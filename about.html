What have I used before (Programming Languages, Tools, Frameworks, Environments)

So this will be a section will be a quick overview of what I have used before as well what I could realistically quickly pick up based on previous experience.

Programming Languages: - C/C++ used interchangeably based on what project requirements, more familiar with C though, as I've used that more for microcontroller/Arduino type projects. - Java used mostly in university courseworks, group courseworks and larger software projects. - Python used mostly for rapid prototyping, eventually was used for my Final Year Project - JavaScript used mostly begrudgingly when doing web development stuff. - TypeScript/Dart used when developing Flutter applications. - Haskell used mostly in university functional programming modules and for my STQL interpreter project. - SQL used during my relational database theory modules - Lua used for general purpose scripting, specifically for Cyberpunk 2077 CyberEngineTweaks mod integration. - Bash used for Linux scripting - Batch used for Windows scripting - ARMv8 assembly used as part of a systems module at one point. - HTML/CSS, I debated putting this as a programming language but someone made a Turing Machine with it so I that makes it count in my book. The BSc Computer Science experience briefly summarized

I wanted to briefly summarize each year of my degree and use that as a jumping off point to list each year's modules and corresponding courseworks.
// psst, my Final Year Project has it's own page here, if you're looking for that

TONE DISCLAIMER: I debated on creating a purely factual list, detailing each module's contents by essentially copying their syllabuses. However, I decided against this as I was strongly inspired by this YouTube video titled "My Whole Computer Science Degree in 12 Minutes". In that video, the creator both recounted his entire degree experience but most importantly, made it a humorous experience rather than a soulless information dump. I wish to replicate this in text form

Year 1

Year 1, this is the year where everyone reaches the peak of "mount stupid" on the dunning-kruger graph. The introductory modules will have you under the impression that you know everything there is to know about whatever topic it is that you're learning. The courseworks will be well-documented and come with extensive test harnesses and they will tell you which libraries to use and how to solve the problems step by step. The excitement of creating new software isn't lost on you, and the power goes to your head, and you will think that you can do anything (you can't).


Year 2

Year 2, this is the year where you fall off "mount stupid" and enter the valley of despair. The modules have stopped holding your hand and you have to start learning these more advanced topics on your own. The courseworks have started to become more open-ended and complex, they stop telling you how to actually solve them and just give you a vague finishing point to reach towards. You start questioning if this is even for you, and you develop your first bout of the classic Computer Science/Developer imposter syndrome.


Year 3

Year 3, if you have somehow not failed Year 2 (difficulty: impossible), then you will have successfully entered Year 3. In this year, you can look forward to doing your final year project/dissertation!! Don't get too happy though, you still have to do some modules, and they have gotten even more complex. You have gotten used to it though, and you are trudging through the slope of enlightenment!
